Data scientist + software dev with a background in physics and planetary science. I love working with remote sensing data and building tools to explore Earth and beyond.

Atmospheric Analysis with Cassini Data

In my most recent work, I developed a data analysis pipeline in Python to identify and measure atmospheric features on Titan using imagery from NASA Cassini's Imaging Science Subsystem.

  • Learn more

Measuring Antarctic Ice Wedges

Using GIS software, I have located and measured ice wedge polygons in Antarctic valley terrain to gain insight into the region's permafrost. Size and other properties of ice wedges can change over time, providing another measure of climate change and its impact on Earth's colder regions.

  • Learn more

Modelling Exoplanet Climates

Global Climate Models can be used to study not only Earth's climate, but also the climates of other planets, even those beyond our own solar system. By studying hypothetical Earth-like planets, we can potentially narrow the search for extraterrestrial life while also gaining a deeper understanding of the complex processes that shape our own climate.

Making Martian Ice in the Lab

I have also enjoyed working hands-on in the lab, fabricating a device that produces ice grains to simulate those on the surface of Mars. I collected and analyzed images of these microscopic grains to study their properties.
